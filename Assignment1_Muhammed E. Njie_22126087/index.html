Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

The man who changed the course of history

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., standing at the Lincoln Memorial, delivers his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech — a defining moment in the fight for civil rights.

Here's a timeline of Dr. King's life:

1929

Born in Atlanta, Georgia

1944

Enters Morehouse College at age 15 under an advanced placement program

1948

Graduates from Morehouse with a degree in sociology and enters Crozer Theological Seminary

1955

Earns a Ph.D. in Systematic Theology from Boston University; helps lead the Montgomery Bus Boycott

1963

Delivers the "I Have a Dream" speech to 250,000 people at the March on Washington

1964

Becomes the youngest recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize at age 35

1968

Assassinated at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee, at the age of 39

"Martin Luther King Jr. taught us that nonviolence is not passive. It is powerful. His unwavering commitment to justice, equality, and love reshaped a nation and continues to inspire the world."
— Former President Barack Obama
